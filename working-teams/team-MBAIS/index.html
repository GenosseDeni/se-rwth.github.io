Model-Based Assistance and Information Services

Working Group

Informatics as a research discipline must constantly face new challenges such as heterogeneous information sources, growing amounts of structural and behavioural data, and broad range of possible end-users. Thus, the need for assistive and information services grows. We meet these challenges by using models and model-driven software engineering as foundation to develop information and assistive systems in the domains controlling and finances, ambient assisted living, health systems, smart energy systems, production and Industry 4.0, sustainable development, Internet of Things (IoT), and smart homes.

Generating software is an important capability for a tooling infrastructure that successfully assists modeling in an agile development process. As every application domain has its own terms, relations, structure and behaviour, there is a need for developing different Domain Specific Languages (DSLs) for each domain and/or purpose. These models can furthermore used for analysis purposes, e.g., of artifacts in complex development projects.

Systems and services for the representation of big amounts of data are commonly used and their representation and system architecture highly depends on the domain in focus. Using generative methods and DSLs accelerates the development process, and decreases development time, as changes of the models are easily passed on to the source code. We are demonstrating the practical application of this approach, e.g., in the MaCoCo and InviDas project using the generator framework MontiGem, when creating digital twin cockpits for Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) or when creating assistive systems.

A step further from informing users is to provide support to them. Supportive or also called assistive systems support human processes both, at work and in private life. It is again an emerging research field regarding the digital transformation of numerous application areas. Such assistance services are commonly embedded in a larger system architecture combining devices and resources, receiving data from sensors and communicate with services. Model-based approaches improve the communication capabilities of these components and ensure consistency. Formal verification methods can be applied to a safety-critical subset of these components and their communication. Moreover, model-based approaches can be used to support privacy-by-design, support variant management, and eases human-centered development approaches.
